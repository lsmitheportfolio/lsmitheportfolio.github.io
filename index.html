Personal Statement

My seventh grade year in my middle/ high school was where I developed as an outstanding student. I was elected to the student government association where I had responsibilities to attend meetings and come to decisions. This was only topped by my acceptance into the National Junior Honors Society. In this year, I got to go Christmas caroling at a retirement home, walk in the March of Dimes to raise money for prematurely born babies, and assist in many school fundraisers.

One year later, my role as a leader took off. I became one of the art directors in the literary club. There, I collected members’ artwork and gave it to the magazine editors to publish. In the National Junior Honors Society, I started and ran a book drive with only the help of two peers. This task was almost as challenging as becoming the secretary of the 4H club where I recorded and summarized each meeting. I furiously strived to be recognized by the National Honors Society, and at the end of the year, I was chosen to be one of the few officers in NJHS. My membership in this club continued until my freshmen year ended. I enjoyed my weekends, volunteering my time to assist the people at Animal Rescue New Orleans. This year tied up nicely.
